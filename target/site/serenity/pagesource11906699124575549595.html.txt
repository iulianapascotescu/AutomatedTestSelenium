<h2 id="mp-tfa-h2" class="mp-h2"><span id="From_today.27s_featured_article"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="From_today's_featured_article">From today's featured article</span></h2>
<div id="mp-tfa"><div id="mp-tfa-img" style="float: left; margin: 0.5em 0.9em 0.4em 0em;">
<div class="thumbinner mp-thumb" style="background: transparent; border: none; padding: 0; max-width: 118px;">
<a href="/wiki/File:Ovulation.jpg" class="image" title="An ovary about to release an egg"><img alt="An ovary about to release an egg" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/39/Ovulation.jpg/118px-Ovulation.jpg" decoding="async" width="118" height="167" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/39/Ovulation.jpg/177px-Ovulation.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/39/Ovulation.jpg/236px-Ovulation.jpg 2x" data-file-width="688" data-file-height="973"></a><div class="thumbcaption" style="padding: 0.25em 0; word-wrap: break-word;">An <a href="/wiki/Ovary" title="Ovary">ovary</a> about to release an egg</div></div>
</div>
<p>The <b><a href="/wiki/Menstrual_cycle" title="Menstrual cycle">menstrual cycle</a></b> is a series of natural changes in <a href="/wiki/Hormone" title="Hormone">hormone</a> production and the structures of the <a href="/wiki/Uterus" title="Uterus">uterus</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ovary" title="Ovary">ovaries</a> of the human <a href="/wiki/Female_reproductive_system" title="Female reproductive system">female reproductive system</a> that make <a href="/wiki/Pregnancy" title="Pregnancy">pregnancy</a> possible. Naturally occurring hormones drive two concurrent and coordinated cycles that last about 28 days. The ovarian cycle controls the production and release of <a href="/wiki/Egg_cell" title="Egg cell">eggs</a> and the release of the hormones <a href="/wiki/Estrogen" title="Estrogen">estrogen</a> and <a href="/wiki/Progesterone" title="Progesterone">progesterone</a>. The uterine cycle governs the preparation and maintenance of the lining of the uterus (<a href="/wiki/Endometrium" title="Endometrium">endometrium</a>) to receive a <a href="/wiki/Fertilisation" title="Fertilisation">fertilized</a> egg. <a href="/wiki/Menstruation" title="Menstruation">Menstruation</a>, a "period" in common parlance, begins on day one of the cycle, and lasts for about five days. Around day fourteen, an egg is usually released from the ovary. Estrogen stimulates the uterus lining to thicken to accommodate an <a href="/wiki/Embryo" title="Embryo">embryo</a> should fertilization occur. If <a href="/wiki/Implantation_(human_embryo)" title="Implantation (human embryo)">implantation</a> does not occur, the lining is released during menstruation.  <a href="/wiki/Menarche" title="Menarche">Menarche</a> (the first period) usually occurs around age twelve, and menstrual cycles usually continue for 3045 years until <a href="/wiki/Menopause" title="Menopause">menopause</a>. (<b><a href="/wiki/Menstrual_cycle" title="Menstrual cycle">Full&nbsp;article...</a></b>)
</p>

<h2 id="mp-tfl-h2" class="mp-h2"><span id="From_today.27s_featured_list"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="From_today's_featured_list">From today's featured list</span></h2>
<div id="mp-tfl"><div id="mp-tfl-img" style="float:right;margin:0.5em 0 0.4em 0.9em;"><div class="thumbinner mp-thumb" style="background: transparent; border: none; padding: 0; max-width: 195px;">
<a href="/wiki/File:Diplomatic_missions_of_Taiwan.svg" class="image" title="Map of countries and territories with current and former Taiwanese diplomatic missions"><img alt="Map of countries and territories with current and former Taiwanese diplomatic missions" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/dd/Diplomatic_missions_of_Taiwan.svg/195px-Diplomatic_missions_of_Taiwan.svg.png" decoding="async" width="195" height="100" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/dd/Diplomatic_missions_of_Taiwan.svg/293px-Diplomatic_missions_of_Taiwan.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/dd/Diplomatic_missions_of_Taiwan.svg/390px-Diplomatic_missions_of_Taiwan.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="2560" data-file-height="1314"></a><div class="thumbcaption" style="padding: 0.25em 0; word-wrap: break-word;">Map of countries and territories with current and former Taiwanese diplomatic missions</div></div>
</div>
<p><b><a href="/wiki/List_of_diplomatic_missions_of_Taiwan" title="List of diplomatic missions of Taiwan">Taiwan, officially the Republic of China, has 112 diplomatic missions</a></b> across the world as of December&nbsp;2020. Due to the <a href="/wiki/One-China_policy" title="One-China policy">One-China policy</a>, other countries are only allowed to maintain relations with either <a href="/wiki/Taiwan" title="Taiwan">Taiwan</a> or the <a href="/wiki/Mainland_China" title="Mainland China">mainland</a>-based <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">People's Republic of China</a>. As most countries have <a href="/wiki/Dates_of_establishment_of_diplomatic_relations_with_the_People%27s_Republic_of_China" title="Dates of establishment of diplomatic relations with the People's Republic of China">changed their recognition</a> to the latter over time, only 17 of Taiwan's diplomatic missions have official status, consisting of fifteen embassies and two consulate-generals. Despite these barriers, 57 <a href="/wiki/United_Nations" title="United Nations">United Nations</a> members maintain relations with Taiwan on an unofficial basis. To serve these locations and other places throughout the world, 92 <a href="/wiki/Taipei_Economic_and_Cultural_Representative_Office" title="Taipei Economic and Cultural Representative Office">semi-official representative offices</a> are utilized for matters that would otherwise be handled by embassies or consulates. Taiwan has established informal representation in China's two <a href="/wiki/Special_administrative_region" title="Special administrative region">special administrative regions</a> as well. In <a href="/wiki/Hong_Kong" title="Hong Kong">Hong Kong</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Taipei_Economic_and_Cultural_Office_in_Hong_Kong" title="Taipei Economic and Cultural Office in Hong Kong">Taipei Economic and Cultural Office in Hong Kong</a> provides services similar to a consulate, while relations to <a href="/wiki/Macau" title="Macau">Macau</a> are handled by the <a href="/wiki/Taipei_Economic_and_Cultural_Office_in_Macau" title="Taipei Economic and Cultural Office in Macau">Taipei Economic and Cultural Office in Macau</a>. (<b><a href="/wiki/List_of_diplomatic_missions_of_Taiwan" title="List of diplomatic missions of Taiwan">Full&nbsp;list...</a></b>)
</p>

<p><b><a href="/wiki/William_Birney" title="William Birney">William Birney</a></b> (May&nbsp;28, 1819&nbsp; August 14, 1907) was a professor, <a href="/wiki/Union_Army" title="Union Army">Union Army</a> general during the <a href="/wiki/American_Civil_War" title="American Civil War">American Civil War</a>, attorney, and author. An ardent <a href="/wiki/Abolitionism_in_the_United_States" title="Abolitionism in the United States">abolitionist</a>, he was one of three superintendents in charge of enlisting colored troops into the Union Army, and in that capacity organized seven regiments. After the war, he established a law practice in Washington, D.C., and after retiring, wrote profusely on religion and history, and authored a biography of his father, <a href="/wiki/James_G._Birney" title="James G. Birney">James G. Birney</a>.
